Title

Introduction to Medical Imaging

Credits

3

Course Coordinator

Klaus Mueller

Current Catalog Description

This course presents an introduction to the mathematical, physical, and computational principles underlying modern medical imaging systems. It will cover fundamentals of X-ray computed tomography, ultrasonic imaging, nuclear imaging, and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), as well as more general concepts required for these, such as linear systems theory and the Fourier Transform. Popular techniques for the visualization, segmentation, and analysis of medical image data will also be discussed, as well as applications of medical imaging, such as image-guided intervention.
Prerequisite

AMS 161 or MAT 132; AMS 210 or MAT 211
Course Goals

Prepare students for employment in the field of medical imaging

Prepare students for graduate study in this or related fields

Augment the knowledge of students working in related fields

Textbook

"Fundamentals of Medical Imaging" by Paul Suetens, Cambridge University Press

Major Topics Covered in Course
  • Week. 1 – Introduction and overview. Historical aspects and application scenarios
  • Week. 2 - Linear systems and Fourier Theory and Image operations
  • Week. 3 – Filters, Multi-scare image enhancement
  • Week. 4 – X-ya physics, Detectors and image acquisition, scanner technology
  • Week. 5 – Reconstruction mathematics, Reconstruction algorithms
  • Week. 6 – Image quality issues, Computational aspects
  • Week. 7 – MRI physics, Interaction with tissue
  • Week. 8 – MRI reconstruction algorithms, Pulse sequences
  • Week. 9 – Image quality issues, Computational aspects, advanced concepts
  • Week. 10 – Nuclear imaging physics, Radionuclides, Detectors and image acquisition, SPECT, PET
  • Week. 11. – Reconstruction mathematics for iterative schemes, Reconstruction algorithms and computational aspects
  • Week. 12. – Ultrasound physics, detectors, and data acquisition, Image generation, 3D reconstruction
